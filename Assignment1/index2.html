ORB.Farm - Core Interactions & Mechanics

List the program's interactive features/mechanics

  • Element Placement – Users can click to place materials like sand, stone, wood, water, and grass into the tank.
  • Species Introduction – Users can introduce living organisms such as daphnia, bacteria, fish, and goldfish by clicking on them from the menu.
  • Waste Decomposition – Bacteria break down organic waste over time, helping maintain the ecosystem's balance.
  • Oxygen and CO₂ Levels – The environment dynamically tracks oxygen and CO₂ levels, which affect the survival of organisms
  • Day-Night Cycle – The background transitions from light to dark in a continuous loop, indicating the passage of time.
  • Fast-Forward Button – Players can click to accelerate time and speed up ecological interactions.
  • Clear Tool – The clear tool removes everything around the cursor area, including organisms, plants, and parts of the tank.
  • Reset Button – Players can click to reset the entire ecosystem and start over from scratch.
  • Pixel-Based Interaction – All interactions occur through a pixel-grid interface, with players clicking directly into the visual tank environment.

ORB.Farm - Genre-Defining Features

What features/mechanics indicate its genre by aligning with its conventions?

  • Set Objectives - The game has no win condition, aligning it with sandbox genre conventions that promote open-ended play.
  • Environmental Simulation - It simulates oxygen and CO₂ levels, waste buildup, and organism survival, fitting within ecosystem simulation norms.
  • Rule-Based Organism Behavior - All lifeforms act according to simple, predefined rules, a staple of artificial life simulations.
  • Cause-and-Effect Interactions - Actions like overpopulating fish or removing plants visibly impact the system, reflecting simulation logic.
  • Emergent System Balance - Stability emerges (or collapses) from user input rather than being scripted, reinforcing the sandbox and A-Life design.
  • Click-Based Interface - The simple, interaction-by-click UI fits within digital toy conventions designed for casual experimentation.

ORB.Farm - Novel Features

What features/mechanics are novel for its genre?

  • Circular Tank Design - Most sandbox simulations use rectangular or open environments, but Orb.Farm limits interaction to a contained circular space, which changes how waste, organisms, and materials settle and interact.
  • Windows 98-Inspired UI (98.css) – The use of a retro operating system aesthetic isn’t just visual; it evokes a digital toy feel and sets it apart from other ecosystem simulators with modern or minimal interfaces.
  • Indirect Ecosystem Management– Unlike simulations where players control species directly, Orb.Farm only allows users to influence the environment, requiring more abstract thinking to maintain balance.
  • Simplified but Functional Chemistry SDay-Night Cycle – The backgystem– Oxygen and CO₂ levels are visually tracked in real time, and although simplified, they have direct effects on life and decay—an elegant representation of complex systems through minimal data.
  • Waste as a Physical Element– Waste accumulates visibly and physically in the environment, sometimes getting trapped in corners or behind obstacles, which adds an extra layer of spatial problem-solving not common in most A-Life simulators.
